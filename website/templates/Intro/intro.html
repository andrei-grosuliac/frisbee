Who are we?

Concordia's Ultimate Frisbee team has been around for 4 years represent our university in multiple leagues and tournament in the Montreal area. Although we are not sponsored or directly affiliated with the School, our club is managed by students from Concordia and we are looking to make our school proud in a pool of competition with other universities and Cegeps across Montreal, Quebec and even Canada. People who join our team are ready to make sacrifices with their school work and free up some time at least once a week for an hour or two to give it their all every single time!


Our typical year

A frisbee year for us starts at the same time as the fall semester where we conduct tryouts and practices on multiple weeks to make up the team for the year. Then, we participate in any designated tournaments and leagues for Universities mainly organized by Ultimate Grand Montreal (UGM) all along the school year until the end of the winter semester where our journey ends. If you never heard of Ultimate Frisbee before

For all you newcomers out there, Ultimate Frisbee is a sport that is similar to american football but without contact. See the video below for a quick overview of the rules.

5 good reasons for playing Ultimate

  1. Non-contact – as a non-contact sport it is a great, safe sport for students of all ages, sizes and abilities.
  2. Self-refereed – the self-refereeing aspect of Ultimate encourages a mature response to competitive situations.
  3. Skill Acquisition – the task of throwing a frisbee accurately and catching it successfully is great for the development of hand-eye co-ordination.
  4. Fun – we regularly find that students who wouldn’t usually get involved in PE really take an interest in Ultimate.
  5. Co-ed – Ultimate is most commonly played with both men and women. Teachers Games, Universtity Games and most leagues in Australia are Mixed.
